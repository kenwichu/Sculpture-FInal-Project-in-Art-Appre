sculp•ture
/'skəlpCHər/
noun
the art of forming solid objects that represent a thing, person, idea, etc. out of a material such as wood, clay, metal, or stone, or an object made in this way.

Famous Sculptors

Michelangelo Buonarroti

Renowned for his mastery of marble, created the iconic sculpture "David," embodying the ideals of Renaissance humanism and classical beauty.

David of Michelangelo

Auguste Rodin

The father of modern sculpture, crafted "The Thinker," a contemplative bronze figure embodying the depth of human thought and emotion.

The Thinker

Gian Lorenzo Bernini

A master of the Baroque era, sculpted "Apollo and Daphne," capturing the dramatic moment of the nymph's transformation into a laurel tree to escape Apollo's pursuit.

Apollo and Daphne

Sculpting Materials

Any material that can be shaped in three-dimensions and are used for shaping, carving, or molding into desired forms.

Commonly used/traditional sculpture materials

Stone
- Througout the history, stone has been the principal sculpture making materials of monumental sculpture. The reason for this:
1. many types of stone are highly resistant to the weather and therefore suitable for external user;
2. stone is available in all parts of the world and can be obtained in large blocks;
3. many stones have a fairly homogenous texture and a uniform hardness that maken them suitable for carving.
