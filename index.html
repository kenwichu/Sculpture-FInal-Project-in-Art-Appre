Famous Sculptors

Michelangelo Buonarroti

Renowned for his mastery of marble, created the iconic sculpture David, embodying the ideals of Renaissance humanism and classical beauty.

Auguste Rodin

The father of modern sculpture, crafted The Thinker, a contemplative bronze figure embodying the depth of human thought and emotion.

Gian Lorenzo Bernini

A master of the Baroque era, sculpted Apollo and Daphne, capturing the dramatic moment of the nymph's transformation into a laurel tree to escape Apollo's pursuit.

Sculpting Materials

Any material that can be shaped in three-dimensions and are used for shaping, carving, or molding into desired forms.

Commonly used/traditional sculpture materials

Stone
- Througout the history, stone has been the principal material for monumental sculpture. The reason for this:
1. many types of stone are highly resistant to the weathering, making them suitable for external user;
2. stone is widely available in all parts of the world and can be obtained in large blocks; and
3. many stones have a fairly homogenous texture and uniform hardness, making them suitable for carving.

Wood
- Wood has also been used by every great civilization and was used extensively udring the Middle Ages. In the 20th century, many sculptors used wood as a medium for carving.
- Both hardwoods and softwoods can be used for sculpting. The grain of the wood is one of its most attractive features, offering a variety of pattern ans and textures to its surfaces. Also, its colors are subtle and varied.
- Wood is used mainly for indoor sculpture, as:
1. it is not as tough or durable as stone;
2. the changes in humidity and temperature may cause its vulnerability to splitting; and
3. it is prone to infestation by insects and fungus.
- In general, wood has a warmth that stone does not have, but it lanks the massive dignity and weight of stone.

Metal
- Wherever metal technology was developed, metal has been used in sculpture.
- They are versatile and durable and has been used by artists for centuries.
They are known for:
1. various techniques such as casting, welding, forging, and fabrication; and
2. finished looks in different ways, including polishing, patination, painting, and texturing, allowing for a variety of aesthetic effects.
- They may require occasional maintenance to prevent corrosion or damage.

Clay
- Is the most common, easily available and cheap of all materials used in sculpting.
Has four(4) characteristics and can be widely used:
1. when wet, it is the mst plastic of all substances, making it easy to shape and capture fine details;
2. when partially dried or completely dried, it becomes firm enough for carving or scraping;
3. when mixed with enough water, it turns to a creamy liquid, perfect for pouring into molds;
4. when burned, it undergoes permanent structural changes, becoming hard and highly durable.

Ivory
- It is a dense, hard substance found in the tusks of mammals.
- It is milky white in color that turn yellow with age.
- Working with it is challenging due to is hardness, requiring high levels of polishing.
- Additionally, the natural shape of itself can be used in sculpting.
